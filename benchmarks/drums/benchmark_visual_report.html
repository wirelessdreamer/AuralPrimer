Current operational policy

The benchmark rule is now sharper: optimize against the trusted rendered suite, keep a manually audited real-audio holdout for sanity checks, and use the normalized Suno corpus only as a diagnostic lens. Diagnostic takeaway

  • The retained retry did improve the old holdout slightly, but the bigger lesson was about reference hygiene, not retry tuning.
  • Manual inspection of King in Zion exposed the start-offset problem; the normalized 7-song diagnostic corpus confirms that this offset was real and recoverable on several songs.
  • The correct follow-up is to keep Suno audio and MIDI in the workflow, but only with safe normalization plus a strict rule that suspect references never become the source-of-truth gate.
Research synthesis

What the literature says and how it maps to this repo

Local synthesis

  • Beat context matters, but the evidence favors soft conditioning rather than hard grid overwrite.
  • Sequence-aware and structure-aware models fit the failure patterns better than isolated frame heuristics.
  • Template and factorization ideas still matter for local overlap resolution.
  • Benchmark hygiene matters as much as model quality. The Suno shootout is now part of that hygiene story.

Primary-source reading that still matters

  • Wu et al. 2018 - A Review of Automatic Drum TranscriptionFrames the main ADT families and reinforces that overlap, class imbalance, and dataset mismatch remain the core failure sources.
  • Vogl et al. 2017 - Drum transcription from polyphonic music with recurrent neural networksSupports sequence-aware decoding. The current kick, snare, and hat failures are sequence failures, not isolated frame errors.
  • Yeh et al. 2023 - Joint Drum Transcription and Metrical Analysis Based on Periodicity-Aware Multi-Task LearningSupports using metrical context as a model input rather than a blunt post-hoc quantizer.
  • Ishizuka et al. 2022 - Global Structure-Aware Drum Transcription Based on Self-Attention MechanismsAdds support for longer-range structure cues, especially for odd meter, repeated sections, and phrase-level pattern continuity.
  • Roebel et al. 2015 - Drum transcription using partially fixed non-negative matrix factorizationKeeps template and factorization methods relevant for local overlap disambiguation, especially kick + hat and snare + hat collisions.
  • Foscarin et al. 2024 - STAR DrumsReinforces that benchmark quality matters. Useful for future external evaluation, but not a replacement for the local rendered suite and audited holdouts.
  • Heyen et al. 2023 - High-Quality and Reproducible Automatic Drum Transcription from Crowdsourced DataAligns with the repo's insistence on reproducible evaluation and static report review after every run.
  • Elhussein et al. 2025 - Enhanced Automatic Drum Transcription via Drum Stem Source SeparationStill emerging, but worth tracking because separation-assisted ADT could reduce overlap failures on dense mixed audio.

Current direction

The retained path is still benchmark-first: keep the rendered trusted suite as the gate, populate the curated real holdout, and use the normalized Suno corpus as a regression-detection layer for importer and timing logic. The new finding is not that Suno MIDI is useless; it is that start normalization rescues part of its value, while the remaining outlier songs prove it still cannot replace validated references.
